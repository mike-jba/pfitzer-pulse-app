# **Framework Overview & Purpose**

* **The Core Objective:** To evaluate customer conversations based on communication quality, relationship-building, professionalism, and the overall customer experience.  
* **The Philosophy:** Customers should never feel like they are calling an "average company". Every interaction must establish a positive experience, emotional connection, confidence, and trust.  
* **The Methodology:** The framework uses the "6 E-Words" to guide a natural, confident, and memorable conversation instead of relying on rigid, robotic scripts.

# **The 6 E-Words Scorecard Structure**

## **Section 1: Enthusiasm**

Focuses on creating a strong first impression, building immediate personal connection, and injecting positive energy into the start of the call.

1. Unique Greeting – Establish a warm, confident first impression that stands out from standard business interactions to project both professionalism and personality.  
   1. **Example:** *"Thank you for calling Pfitzer Pest Control, this is Jil, how may I make your day awesome today?"*  
2. Positive Energy & Tone – Projects friendliness, positivity, and confidence through vocal tone to directly influence how the customer feels emotionally.  
3. Ask Customer's Name – Personalizes the interaction from the outset to begin building familiarity and a working relationship.  
4. Double Intro \- Opening – The CSR naturally repeats their own name later during the initial intake to reinforce familiarity, trust, and connection.  
   1. **Example:** *"Absolutely, my name is Jil by the way, and I'll be helping you with this today."*

## **Section 2: Engage**

Focuses on thoroughly understanding the customer's issues and showing authentic intent to help, rather than rushing directly into pricing.

5. Ask Questions About the Issue (3-5 Questions) – Actively uses multiple discovery questions to gather deep context, build trust, and avoid sounding purely transactional.  
   1. **Examples:** *"Where are you seeing the ants?"* | *"How long has this been happening?"* | *"Have you noticed activity inside or outside?"*  
6. Show Interest in the Customer – Demonstrates curiosity, attentiveness, and total involvement in what the customer is experiencing so they feel heard and important.

## **Section 3: Empathy**

Focuses on humanizing the interaction through genuine emotional connection, comfort, and rapport.

7. Connect with the Customer / Build Rapport – Identifying small, unforced moments to humanize the conversation without derailing the purpose of the call.  
   1. **Examples:** *"Hope you're having a good day."* | *"What kind of dog is that?"* | *"Congratulations on the new home."*  
8. Validate the Customer – Acknowledges the customer's specific frustrations, worries, or situations in a deeply caring, professional manner.  
   1. **Examples:** *"Oh wow, that sounds frustrating."* | *"That definitely doesn't sound good."* | *"I can imagine that's uncomfortable."*  
9. Use Customer's Name During the Call – Fluidly weaving the customer’s name throughout the dialogue to strengthen continuous personalization and focus.

## **Section 4: Encourage**

Focuses on validating the customer's choice, reducing hesitation, and making them feel fully supported and reassured.

10. Use Positive Words – Intentionally replacing negative wording with encouraging, high-vibe language.  
    1. **Approved Words:** *Absolutely* , *Definitely* , *Beautiful* , *Wonderful* , *Perfect*.  
11. Reassure with Confidence – Actively instilling ultimate confidence in the company’s capability, process, and resolution safety.  
    1. **Examples:** *"You called the right place."* | *"We'll take great care of you."* | *"We handle this all the time."*

### Sub-Branch: Booking Strategy

A specialized commercial execution subset under Encourage designed to secure the commitment.

12. Gather Customer Information – Confidently and professionally collecting core operational fields:  
    1. Full Address  
    2. Email Address  
    3. Payment Information  
    4. Phone Number  
    5. Scheduling Details  
13. Upgrade List / Fast-Track Support – Proactively offering advanced scheduling opportunities or secondary premium choices where relevant.  
14. Hail Mary – Deploying a final, polished attempt to preserve and secure a booking before letting the customer hang up.

## **Section 5: Educate**

A foundational training vertical for Pfitzer Pest Control designed to project value, professional authority, and process safety before presenting price.

15. Share Your Goal – Explicitly articulating your positive intent and structural roadmap at the start of the interaction.  
    1. **Example:** *"My goal today is to answer your questions and help you find the best solution for your home."*  
16. Deliver Company Value Proposition – Presenting clear reasons why the company is credible, local, or uniquely reliable:  
    1. Years in business / experience  
    2. Local/family-owned identity  
    3. Core professionalism and dedicated customer care  
17. Explain Application Style & Treatment Areas – Walking the customer through how treatment is physically deployed and where it goes to establish absolute clarity.  
18. Explain Products & Safety – Providing professional assurance regarding chemical applications, covering family/pet safety, drying windows, and immediate outcomes.  
19. Set Expectations / Explain Process with Technician – Outlining operational next steps so the customer feels completely prepared:  
    1. Technician arrival windows and timelines  
    2. Preparation instructions and follow-up processes  
20. Offer Proposal \+ Offer Price – Confidently delivering the financial quote, ensuring that value education always precedes the actual cost discussion.

## **Section 6: Extra Mile**

Focuses on exceeding transactional requirements through deliberate secondary support and warm, polished closures.

21. Offer Additional Services – Looking for secondary client issues or introducing wider product catalogs.  
    1. **Examples:** *"Are you seeing anything else around the property besides ants?"* | *"Just so you know, we also provide lawn care services."*  
22. Offer or Review Membership Benefits – Introducing or confirming continuous service programs and recurring membership perks.  
23. Express Gratitude & Close Professionally – Concluding the interaction with sincere appreciation and elite professional warmness.  
24. Double Outro \- Closing – Ensuring the representative's name is repeated naturally one last time before disconnecting to cement the relationship.  
    1. **Example:** *"Thank you again, my name is Jil if you need anything else."*
